GMO: 2 Slides of a Leaf

Written and Edited by Daksh ParikhPosted on 15 DEC, 2020 - 3 min read

Genetically Modified Crops has been a hot topic of discussion and debate for world forums since its advent. It has a series of disadvantages and advantages which puts us in a situation wherein we are forced to choose the lesser of both evils.

Increasing human population is a matter of concern and has a lot of consequences on the environment besides the obvious stress on natural resources to fulfil the ever-growing human needs. Genetically Modified Food is food produced by altering their DNA through the means of genetic engineering. This process manipulates their natural traits and allows us to modify them in ways which we desire.

Looking at the brighter side of GMOs let’s talk about its positives:

1) It helps increase the yield of the seed which helps the farmer to increase his harvest per acre and also earn more.
2) By virtue of genetic engineering it makes the crop resistant to parasites, pests and decreases its vulnerability of going bad.
3) Genetic modifying can also help in changing the nutritional values.
4) Genetic modification can also improve the visual appearance and size of the harvest which makes it easy to market and fetch higher prices.

There’s a broad scientific consensus that GMOs currently on the market pose no threat to human health. But still the use of GMOs is a very controversial topic. There’s a chunk of people who demand a tight check on the production and usage of these GMOs as most of them are patented by large corporations or companies.

Looking at the flip side of GMOs let's take a glance on its negatives:

1) Genetic modification increases the risk of disruption of the biodiversity and ecosystem because the “better” trait produced by modifying genes can favour one organism. Hence, it can disrupt the natural gene flow.
2) There is always a constant threat lingering on human life due to modified or artificial nutritional value.
3) The transgenic crops not only endanger not only farmers but the trade, and the environment as well.
4) It results in increased price of cultivation and has an agenda of marketisation of farmed goods
5) The excessive production of genetically altered foods will be rendered ineffective over time because the parasites that used to deter from these might develop resistance against them over time

What this means for us?

GMOs are still to be deemed as a boon or a bane for humanity. Yet, the basic conscience forces us to think whether altering the natural characteristics of food which has allowed us to thrive and evolve for millions years is a healthy practice or not. But as Gandhi once said “The world has enough for everyone’s need, but not for anyone’s greed”. A lot of environmentalists have suggested that if we make small self sufficient food communities the soil can grow enough natural food to sustain everyone. “Innovations that are guided by smallholder farmers, adapted to local circumstances, and sustainable for the economy and environment are going to be necessary to ensure food safety in the future.”
- BILL GATES.
