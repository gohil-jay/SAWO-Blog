BIRD FLU: HATCHING ANOTHER INFLUENZA?

Written by Diya Shah & Edited by Dhruvi GathaniPosted on 14 JAN, 2021 - 3 min read

With the existence of the terrifying Coronavirus and the resulting outbreak and crumbling economy, the presence of Bird flu has alarmed humans. Bird flu or Avian influenza is caused because of the virus present within wild aquatic animals. It is circulated when it comes in contact with a bird’s excretion through its nose, mouth or eyes. This disease can easily infect domestic birds, which are then commercialised.

The transmission of this flu wasn’t under anyone’s radar during its origin – which was only in two states, Maharashtra and Madhya Pradesh in 2005-06. Therefore, there was a panic when it resurfaced and spread in around 10 states. Gujarat, Haryana, HP, Kerala, MP, Rajasthan and UP have confirmed the existence of this flu while Chhattisgarh, Delhi and Maharashtra still are under observation.

The question that pops up after such a challenging year due to the pandemic is - are all viruses and flus contagious and will all of them jeopardise a human’s life? Well, no. The facts state that the labourers in contact with the migrated birds have been infected only through its direct contact or through the surface on which the infected bird(s) have been kept, but it has NOT resulted in infecting a human without direct contact. This mild outbreak does not mean all the migration and import of birds can be ceased, which would take a major toll on the poultry business. The question of hygiene and safety hovers over the poultry sector, whose growth had boosted during the pandemic.

Albeit all the infected creatures have been destroyed, humans are filled with paranoia causing misinformation to turn into a warped reality. But perfectly cooked meat or eggs cooked with utmost hygienic measures do not transmit this flu. The pandemics and epidemics that have been surfacing throughout decades now impact humans’ mental and physical health in more ways than one. Moreover, the impending pandemic has etched into individuals’ brains that all viruses and flus would absolutely ruin their lifestyle and turn it upside down. But the reality is that the avian influenza has hardly touched a human in the absence of a source of direct contact.

The economy of poultry is left at stake with this exaggeration that the bird flu infects all the food that more than half the country is consuming. Therefore, a keen observation on the disease with utmost precautions should be ensured, as even a little nick of misinformation would send the business downhill. The bottom line here is that surely not all viruses and flus are severe, if taken precautions accordingly and attentively.
